Malapuram



City in Kerala

Malappuram is a city in the Indian state of Kerala, spread over an area of 58.20 kmÂ². The first municipality in the district formed in 1970, Malappuram serves as the administrative headquarters of Malappuram district. Divided into 40 electoral wards, the city has a population density of 1,742 per square kilometre.



Top Three Destination

Teak Museum

Teak Museum is located 4 km from Nilambur, a town in the Malappuram district of Kerala, South India. Teak occurs naturally in India with the main teak forests found in Kerala

Adyanpara Falls

Adyanpara Falls is a cascading waterfall in the Kurmbalangodu village of Nilambur taluk in Kerala, India. It is 14 kilometres from Nilambur town, and attracts tourists from various parts of Kerala.

Nedumkayam Rain Forest

Nedumkayam Rain Forest contains Teak trees & an elephant training center
